03. Alteryx Data Analysis

This project focuses on analysing global temperature data from 1950 onwards to uncover significant climatic trends. Utilizing a comprehensive dataset, we leverage Alteryx for data manipulation and analysis. Key objectives include identifying cities with average temperatures of 25 degrees Celsius or higher, determining the country with the highest number of such warm cities, and discovering the years with the highest and lowest average global temperatures. Through systematic workflows and detailed analysis, we aim to gain valuable insights into global temperature dynamics and trends.

05. Optimal Portfolio Construction

The project aimed to construct an optimized portfolio using three assets, Asset_01, Asset_05, and Asset_11, to maximize the risk-adjusted return, represented by the Sharpe Ratio. Python optimization techniques were employed to calculate the optimal weights of individual assets, ensuring efficient allocation within set constraints. Two strategies were explored: a fully invested portfolio and a Long-Short strategy. Results showed slightly better outcomes with the Long-Short approach, highlighting the significance of diverse optimization methods in portfolio construction.

06. Fund Classification using Python

The project delves into feature extraction methods implemented on a dataset comprising 200 funds, segregated into four distinct categories. Its primary focus is to evaluate the performance of three classification algorithms - Random Forest Classifier, Support Vector Machine, and Multi-Layer Perceptron Classifier - in accurately categorizing these funds into their respective labels. Additionally, the project conducts a comparative assessment of the predictive scores and confusion matrices generated by each classification model, shedding light on their efficacy in fund categorization.

07. Derivative Mishaps Case Study

The Amaranth Case Study delves into the downfall of Amaranth Advisors LLC, a notable hedge fund managed by trader Brian Hunter. Renowned for his bold trading strategies in the natural gas markets, Hunter's aggressive bets on natural gas futures led to catastrophic losses of approximately $6 billion in 2006. This report examines the root cause of the risk management failure, tracing the sequence of events leading to the fund's collapse. Furthermore, it analyzes the repercussions of this failure on various stakeholders within Amaranth Advisors LLC. Additionally, the report offers personal insights and reflections on the discussed case.
